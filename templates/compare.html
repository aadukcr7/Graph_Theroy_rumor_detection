Rumor versus organic propagation

The same graph-theoretic lens shows two very different diffusion patterns. Rumors are faster and more centralized, while organic information spreads more gradually.

Rumor Example

Fast diffusion
  • Star-like or hub-and-spoke structure
  • High centralization and high max degree
  • Small diameter and short propagation delay
  • Thicker edges because temporal weights are larger

Organic Example

Slow diffusion
  • Path-like or sparse chain structure
  • Lower centralization and lower hub dominance
  • Larger diameter and longer propagation delay
  • Thinner edges because temporal weights are smaller

What changes between the two?

TopologyRumor is compact and radial; organic is elongated and chain-like.
SpeedRumor reaches many nodes quickly; organic spreads more slowly.
CentralityRumor concentrates activity around one source; organic distributes activity across the path.
Temporal weightRumor edges have smaller delays and therefore higher weights.
